Paper Prototype

I used low-fidelity prototyping in the early stages of design. Using paper, sticky notes, scissors, tape, I create a paper-prototype of my project solution. Focus on tasks and interactions.
There are six main tasks:
  1. sign up
  2. jump to “homepage”
  3. click “borrow”
  4. click “community”
  5. click “message”
  6. click “me”

Design Defense

Connecting the borrower and lender is the critical feature of my application because it directly speaks to the intent of the solution. The quick and good match of needs of borrowing things and ability of offering help is the reason why residents in Courtyard want to use this app.

I came up with three approaches for this feature:
  1. Users list all the things they can lend in pre-set items category. Whoever want to borrow such item just click and select.
  2. Users write borrow-needs posts in the pre-set goods category pages. Posts with highest given-out points is on the top. Whoever wants to lend can see all the posts.
  3. Users list all the items they can lend on their own page. Whoever wants to borrow use search and reach certain pages.

I used three criteria to determine on the final approach.
  • Require less user effort.
  • Differentiate needs in emergency or not
  • Encourage user participation
